Influence

How culture, media, and technology shaped society

A Short History of Influence

Influence has shifted dramatically from traditional media to digital platforms. Between the 1990s and 2020s, the world moved from TV and magazines to social media, algorithms, and global online communities.

Each decade introduced new ways for ideas, trends, and personalities to spread.

1990s

The 90s were dominated by television, magazines, radio, and early internet forums. Celebrities, musicians, and TV hosts shaped global culture. Advertising was bold, colorful, and heavily brand‑driven.

Subcultures like grunge, hip‑hop, and skate culture spread through music videos and printed media.

2000s

The 2000s introduced the rise of the internet: blogs, early social networks, and viral videos. MySpace, YouTube, and online forums created the first wave of digital influencers.

Celebrity culture peaked, with paparazzi and tabloids shaping public perception.

2010s

The 2010s were the era of social media dominance. Instagram, Twitter, and YouTube created global influencers, content creators, and viral trends.

Algorithms began shaping what people saw, creating new forms of digital fame.

2020s

The 2020s are defined by TikTok, AI‑generated content, micro‑influencers, and hyper‑personalized feeds. Influence is now decentralized and global.

Digital creators, streamers, and virtual personalities shape culture in real time.
