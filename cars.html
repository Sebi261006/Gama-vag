Cars

From raw mechanics to AI-driven mobility

A Short History of Cars

The evolution of cars reflects the evolution of society itself. From early mechanical machines built for durability and simplicity, to the aerodynamic, computer‑controlled vehicles of today, each decade brought new priorities and new technologies.

In the 20th century, cars symbolized freedom and status. By the 2000s, they became technological showcases. Today, they are transitioning into intelligent, electric, and sustainable mobility platforms.

1990s

The 90s were dominated by Japanese tuner culture — lightweight cars with high‑revving engines, manual transmissions, and huge aftermarket scenes. Icons like the Toyota Supra MK4, Nissan Skyline R34, Mazda RX‑7 FD, and Honda NSX defined the decade.

Prices were relatively accessible: a performance car cost around 15,000–25,000 USD, while the average salary was much lower than today. This made tuning culture explode.

2000s

The 2000s brought the supercar boom: Ferrari Enzo, Porsche Carrera GT, Lamborghini Murciélago, and the legendary Bugatti Veyron. Technology improved dramatically — stability control, early infotainment, and better safety systems.

Prices rose sharply. A supercar cost 150,000–300,000 USD, while the average income grew slowly. Cars became luxury statements.

2010s

The 2010s marked the beginning of the electric revolution. Tesla disrupted the industry with the Model S, while hybrid and futuristic cars like the BMW i8 pushed boundaries. Performance cars like the Nissan GT‑R also defined the decade.

Prices increased moderately, but EVs were still expensive. Governments offered incentives to make them more accessible.

2020s

The 2020s are defined by electrification, autonomy, and minimalist design. Cars are becoming software platforms with over‑the‑air updates, AI assistance, and advanced sensors. Models like the Porsche Taycan, Tesla Model Y, BMW iX, and Kia EV6 represent the new era.

Prices are at an all‑time high due to inflation and technology costs. EVs range from affordable compact models to premium luxury vehicles.
